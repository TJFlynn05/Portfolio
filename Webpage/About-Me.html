    • About Me

    You may ask who am I when coming across my website, my name is Tyler, I am in year 2 of my undergraduate degree in Games Design and Programming at university and here's a bit about myself:

    I didn't realise I would end up into programming until year 9 in highschool during the Covid-19 lockdown and I participated in a closed beta for double eleven ,this led me to be part of the development cycle of a game and I instantly fell in love with the process of getting the community involved in making a game that will be enjoyed by millions. I started to talk to the developers asking what role they had in development and I found out that they were a programmer. I talked to them about it for a while and it sparked my interest in programming, this led me to start learning c++, it was quite difficult at first but I managed to do some basic input and output to the console, anything more complex than that and it became too much for me to understand so I stopped for a while but stayed in contact and asked more questions to the developer.

    Fast forward to college I took a course that was [insert here] which was coursework based and the engine we used was unreal engine I have to admit I struggled at first with this too but I managed to get a basic understanding of the tools and how to use them to design and decorate levels and as I started to understand the work I produced became better and looked more refined and this filled me with hope of the future. During the first year of college I did quite well up until the FMP (Final Major Project) where we had to work in a team to produce a game, the game idea was overscoped because I was unaware of how much work it would take and where everyone's skill levels were at, as deadline drew closer I was more focused on the programming but also trying to help out the team and fill gaps in where I could, this led to me not writing up on the work and this then led to a referral which then limited me to a pass for the first year. This affected me a lot and it put me down for a bit but as soon as second year came around I was determined to do better and I did, I managed to keep on top of my work and producing better quality work and by the end of the year I managed to make a game, which wasn't very mechanically complex but it was a challenge for me to produce this game as I overscoped intentionally with this project as I wanted to challenge myself to see what I could do and how much of the main game idea I could produce in the time frame. Ideas did have to be cut from the final game but I was happy with what I produced as I messed around with things I thought I could only dream of making. When it came to results day I was very stressed but I felt it was going to be better than last year, I opened the envelope and I got a distinction which led me into pursuing a degree in Games Design and Programming at university as I knew this was the career path I wanted to go down.
